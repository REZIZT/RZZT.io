	RZZT (prounounced "rezizt") is a cypherpunk collective
	dedicated to the development of secure, privacy-enriching
	Free-Libre Open Source Software (FLOSS) applications_

	RZZT is a supporting member of both the Free Software Foundation
	(FSF) and the Electronic Frontier Foundation (EFF), who both
	fight for the user_

	To better represent the project to the masses, provide avenues
	for funding for projects, and defend free licenses, RZZT is
	in the process of UK-nonprofit registration_


	Projects
	───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

	In addition to running various nodes to support Bitcoin, Tor,
	Electrum and Bitmessage, RZZT sponsors and contributes to the
	development of several FLOSS applications and games for
	different platforms.

	Libre
	───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

	Free(libre), open source software (FOSS/FLOSS) is important.
	Anyone can crack open the code and see what it does, modify
	it, and share it with the world. But most importantly, it
	can be audited and prove that it is not doing anything
	malicious.

	RZZT developers work in completely FLOSS environments and are
	strong supporters of libre software.



	Free
	───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

	All expenses for development of RZZT applications are paid in
	full by generous donations from third parties and the continued
	support from the RZZT collective.



	Privacy / Decentralization
	───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

	RZZT applications are built from the ground up around user
	privacy, with the assumption that local networks and ISPs
	(either maliciously or by overreaching mandate) may be spying
	on your traffic, inclined to inject malicious content, and that
	your well-being is at risk from oppressive regimes.

	By building with extreme situations in mind, we raise the bar
	for user expectations.



	Security
	───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

	Perfect security does not exist, but the next best thing is
	actively developed and patched open source code, publicly
	auditable contributions, and regular audits.

	RZZT strives to keep security and privacy standards high, and
	to do right by its users with its transparent policies.

	RZZT applications are written with the assumption that they
	will eventually be exploited, and that the challenge to keep
	users safe will be an endless battle requiring constant
	vigilence.



	Freedom
	───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

	Applications that depend on centralized services often
	bottleneck users into insecure, privacy invasive and
	compromising situations. This can lead to situations of data
	theft, fraud, censorship, and privacy invasion by overreaching
	and too-big-to-care service providers.



	Censorship-resistant
	───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

	Censorship resistance is an important part of RZZT's mission.
	While self-censorship and voluntary Web-of-Trust (WoT) models
	are useful in reducing fraud and spam inside networks, blanket
	censorship has no place in free software.

	The RZZT collective consists of developers, designers,
	translators, consultants, security professionals, researchers,
	and contributors from every corner of the globe.

	Each RZZT project has its own unique contributors that may
	overlap with others. In general, the core team is involved in
	development of most, if not all, RZZT projects.

	The national nodes are individuals who volunteer their support
	to host events, research, file paperwork, advertise locally,
	or even run service nodes on behalf of RZZT and its projects.

	RZZT helps projects succeed by funding their development costs.
	The success or failure of many useful, innovative and
	interesting projects depends on adequate funding.

	By keeping our administrative costs to a minimum, we ensure
	that as much income as possible is directed toward developing
	open-source software — we don't waste time or money.

	Although RZZT has no real expenses, the projects we support do.
	Development of Taskhive is expected to cost US $1500
	(about £1200, €1400 or AU $2000) per month until the end of 2017.

	If just 300 FLOSS-lovers made monthly donations of US $5 RZZT
	would be able to entirely fund Taskhive's development. This
	would take the pressure off the handful of individuals who are
	currently spending significant amounts from their own pockets
	to ensure the project can be professionally developed.

	We don't want projects that have enormous potential like
	Taskhive to fail simply because they had to rely on
	unsustainable funding models.

	You can do your part to donate at Patreon.com/RZZT
